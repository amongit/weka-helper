It wasn't Barack Obama the preacher or Obama the proselytizer who addressed the nation Monday night. It was Obama the professor, as he took a relatively dry, academic and measured approach to the furor in Ferguson, Missouri. Obama pleaded for calm in the aftermath of a grand jury's decision not to indict Darren Wilson, a white Ferguson police officer, for the fatal shooting of Michael Brown, an unarmed African American teen ager, last August.
