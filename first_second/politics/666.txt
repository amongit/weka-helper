“Selma” director Ava DuVernay may not have won a Golden Globe Sunday night, but for her even to have made it to the director’s chair is an achievement.
In 2014, 93 percent of the 250 top grossing domestic films did not have a female director, according to a new report by the Center for the Study of Women in Television and Film at San Diego State University. Women were vastly underrepresented in other positions as well.
