Jobs Index Improved, Then Plateaued in 2014
Gallup's Job Creation Index closed 2014 at +27, an eight point increase since the beginning of that year. The index plateaued in May and, despite a slight increase in September, has remained mostly constant.
The slow but steady increase from the Great Recession in 2008 and 2009 has been the subject of much political debate, and will surely remain a key issue for Americans heading toward the 2016 presidential election.
Most workers now say their employers are hiring or not changing, as opposed to letting people go.
